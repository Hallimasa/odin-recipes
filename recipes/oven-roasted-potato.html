Oven Roasted Potato

A fried cheese ball is a savory appetizer made by mixing together cheese, breadcrumbs, herbs, and other seasonings, forming them into small balls, and then deep frying them until crispy and golden brown on the outside, and gooey and melt on the inside. The cheese used can vary depending on personal preference, but popular choices include mozzarella, cheddar, or a mixture of different cheeses. Once fried, the cheese balls are often served hot and crispy with a dipping sauce, such as marinara, ranch, or aioli, to add extra flavor and creaminess. The result is a delicious and satisfying snack that is perfect for sharing with friends or family.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • ½ teaspoon dried basil
  • ½ teaspoon dried marjoram
  • ½ teaspoon dried dill weed
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon dried parsley
  • ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 475 degrees F (245 degrees C).
  2. Combine oil, garlic, basil, marjoram, dill, thyme, oregano, parsley, pepper flakes, and salt in a large bowl.

  3. Stir in potatoes until evenly coated. Place potatoes in a single layer on a roasting pan or baking sheet.

  4. Roast in the preheated oven, turning occasionally to brown on all sides, until tender, about 20 to 30 minutes.

  5. Serve it !
